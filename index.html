Mental health is your emotional and psychological wellbeing. It affects how you think, feel, and act each day especially under stress and shapes how you cope with challenges, make decisions, and connect with others. It includes managing emotions, staying mentally clear, building resilience, maintaining self-worth, and supporting healthy relationships and routines like sleep and motivation.


Everyone has mental health, and it can change over time. Simple habits can help, but if struggles start affecting daily life for a while, getting professional support is normal, practical, and worthwhile.

Anxiety

Everyone worries sometimes before a test, a match, a presentation, or a big conversation. Anxiety becomes a problem when the worry feels constant, intense, or hard to control, even when things are “fine.”

Depression

Depression isn’t just “being sad.” It can look like feeling low for a long time, losing interest in things you normally enjoy, or feeling empty or numb. Some teens don’t feel sad—they feel tired, irritated, or disconnected.

Bipolar Disorder

Bipolar disorder involves extreme changes in mood and energy. It usually includes periods of depression and periods of unusually high energy called mania (or a milder form called hypomania). Not everyone who has mood swings has bipolar disorderthis is more intense and lasts longer than typical ups and downs.
